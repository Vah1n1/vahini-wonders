about

Curiosity
as a discipline.

I'm Vahini, a pseudonym for a teenager in the UK who is deeply, genuinely obsessed with physics and what the universe is actually made of.

It started with clouds. I've always thought someone painted them, and I got obsessed with figuring out the technique. Turns out it's fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, and light scattering. Turns out it's the same physics that tells you what a star is made of from 400 light years away. That's the thread I keep pulling — through citizen science, through building things, through writing, through code. Through weekly tutorials with an Oxford Physics student on the COMPOS programme, where I've been since Year 10

Sometimes the pulling produces something. A paper. A simulation. A spectrum captured from a suburban garden. But the questions always come first.

Vahini is an alias. All writing and projects are original work.
